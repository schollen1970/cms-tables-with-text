Loc-Line® Modular Hose System:     {Product specifications after part #/costing tables}

A customized hose solution for a wide variety of commercial and industrial applications, utilizing standard (modular) components. Originally designed for the machine tool industry to deliver coolant at the exact location of cutting tool and target metal. Fittings consist of a ball and socket assembly with enough friction to create unlimited positioning, full flow thru that will not fatigue, remains in a set position, and customizable with standard/modular components. Uses have grown into the electronic, scientific, graphic arts, printing, woodworking and aquatic industries.

Use in any situation that requires precise, yet adjustable, directional flow of fluid, air, vacuum or third-arm mechanical positioning. Four hose diameters: 1/4”, 1/2”, 3/4” and a larger 2-1/2” to accommodate many applications.

• ball and socket design is fully hand adjustable in position and length;

• bending does not affect the inside diameter, thus not decreasing flow, plus will not kink or fatigue;

• inherently non-conductive;

• manufactured from a premium acetal copolymer material which is chemically resistant to petroleum products, coolants and some common chemicals. Selected components also available in an acid resistant polyester;

Typically blue and orange colors; some optional gray or black colors available for specific components. Optional acid-resistant polyester components in yellow color.

Commonly asked questions:

Coolant hose drains while I change my pallet or tooling. How can I stop this from happening?

Install a check valve in the hose line to eliminate dripping.

 

Can I put a connector on both ends?

Utilize a double socket to reverse the direction of the hose and add the second connector.

 

How can I add hose support mid-way in a coolant line?

Install a T-fitting, which features a mounting boss to anchor the hose to a fixed support.

 

How can I get a nozzle to run coolant parallel to my work?

Utilize a swivel nozzle with an adjustable spray head (featuring a 40.degree range of motion over the flared base). The evenly spaced holes create an even distribution of coolant or air.

 

What is the best way to make a quick turn?

Install a 90.degree nozzle for a quick turn close to the cutting tool. 90.degree nozzles can also be assembled directly on a NPT fitting for tight spaces.

 

Coolant tends to head to the edges. How can I improve the distribution?

Utilize a straight flow nozzle, designed with a wider center and straight edges to create a more uniform flow over the width of the nozzle.

 

How can I attach non-system nozzles or accessories?

Install a socket x NPT adapter to thread on your other accessories.

 

How can we produce a smooth, thin pattern?

Utilize a flat nozzle, available with slots or holes, to create the perfect thin spraying pattern.

 

How do I spread fluid or air over a wider area?

Utilize a flare nozzle; four widths available to accommodate the application.

 

How do I regulate flow?

Install a valve in-line.

 

Need to increase the pressure on the system. Is that possible?

By utilizing clamps (1/4” and ½” hose size) to tighten joints to increase pressure.

 

Can I create multiple output lines from a single source line?

Install a Y-fitting to create two (or additional) lines. Also possible to use a reducing Y-fitting to drop the branch line sizes.

 

How can we reduce the total number of joints within a given system?

Use of the extended elements and/or extended elbows. Can also be utilized with the higher pressure clamps.

 

Possible to replace flared copper tubing runs?

Install a flare nut adapter fitting (very popular in air applications in the printing industry).
